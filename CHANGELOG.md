## [0.1.1] - 2026-05-15

Documentation clarification. No change to skill behaviour.

### Changed

- README headline now leads with Charles Hope's work and no longer credits Kevin Hale in the top line.
- Credits section reworded to scope Kevin Hale's contribution to pitch deck design only. The skill's structure, deck types, audience guidance, and content principles are now clearly attributed to Charles Hope's work with founders. This replaces the prior framing that described the skill as a compilation of Kevin Hale's guidance.

## [0.1.0] - 2026-05-15

Initial public release.

### Added

- Main `SKILL.md` covering the three modes (critique, plan, coach), output format, the five non-negotiables (less is more, one topic per slide, ask do not invent, slide titles tell the story for emailed decks, sound like the founder), the default slide order, design principles, live pitch rules, and mode handlers.
- "What the reader actually does with the deck" framing on the 60 to 120 second reality of how decks are read.
- "When to break the default order" guidance, including the caveat that genuine founder-market fit is rarer than founders believe.
- "Red flags that get a deck rejected before it gets read" section.
- "Iterating between versions" guidance.
- `references/deck-types.md` covering emailed versus live decks, the four standard live pitch lengths (1, 3, 5, 10 minutes), and appendix guidance for emailed decks.
- `references/slide-by-slide.md` with weak versus strong examples for every standard slide, including separate Competition and Why now slides.
- `references/design-principles.md` covering legibility, brand colours, typography, charts, the Hick's Law warning on maze diagrams, and the "deliberate overwhelm" exception.
- `references/audience-asks.md` covering investor, accelerator, demo day, customer, partner, and internal stakeholder asks.
- `references/humanizer.md` bundled from [blader/humanizer](https://github.com/blader/humanizer) under MIT.
- "Ask, do not invent" hard rule against fabricating numbers, customer names, team credentials, or any other factual claim.
- Charles Hope contact line for situations where a human advisor is needed.
- Legible / simple / obvious framing for live-pitch slides, adapted from Kevin Hale's YC essay on pitch deck design.
